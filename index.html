Flexbox

What is a flex?

Flex is used to create whole module or a block that can wrap elements into it. Positioning in vertical or horizontal stack is possible with flex. A flex's alignment is identified along the main axis and cross axis. Main axis is generally horizontal, but it can be made vertical by using flex-direction property. Flex is responsive and flexible by itself, and it aligns the flex items in the right position with respect to the other flex items in the flex. The grids below are flex items wrapped inside their flex-box or flex-container.

A flex is defined with the display property. IE 10 and IE 11 offers partial support to flex when prefixed with -ms-. Android 4.3 provides partial support with -webkit- prefix. Edge, Safari, Chrome, Firefox, Opera and Android browsers above 4.3 provides support even to the recent updates of flex.

flex-wrap

We make use of flex-wrap which is similar to the wrapping options in any text editor to wrap the contents of the flex-box in the next line. The flex-box auto adjusts the spacing and renders the flex-items in the next line. By default, we have the nowrap active, which doesn't wrap the flex-items of the flex-box.

justify-content

This is used to align the flex-box contents along the main axis. This has five property values: flex-start, flex-end, center, space-around and space-between.

  1. flex-start: Pushes flex-items to the starting of where the flex-box starts.
  2. flex-end: Aligns flex-items to the end where the flex-box ends.
  3. center: Adjusts the space to align the flex-items to the exact center of the flex-box.
  4. space-around: Adjusts space around the flex-items in the flex-box.
  5. space-between: Adjusts space between the flex-items in the flex-box.

align-items

Align-items is used to align the flex-items along the vertical or cross axis. This has five property values: flex-start, flex-end, center, baseline and stretch.

  1. flex-start: Pushes flex-items to the starting of where the flex-box starts in the cross-axis.
  2. flex-end: Aligns flex-items to the end where the flex-box ends in the cross axis.
  3. center: Adjusts the space to align the flex-items to the exact center of the flex-box in the cross axis.
  4. baseline: Aligns the flex-items along the baseline of the flexbox.
  5. stretch: Stretches the flex-items along the cross axis.

align-content

Align-items is used to align the flex-items along the vertical or cross axis. This has six property values: flex-start, flex-end, center, space-around, space-between and stretch.

  1. flex-start: Pushes flex-containers content/lines to the starting of where the flex-box starts in the cross-axis.
  2. flex-end: Aligns flex-container lines/content to the end where the flex-box ends in the cross axis.
  3. center: Adjusts the space to align the flex-container content to the exact center of the flex-box in the cross axis.
  4. space-between: Makes space between the content/lines in the flex-container in the flex-box
  5. space-around: Makes space around the content/lines in the flex-container in the flex-box
  6. stretch: Stretches the flex-container content along the cross axis.
